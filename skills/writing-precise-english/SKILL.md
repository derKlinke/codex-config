# Writing Precise English

Write in crisp standard English. Cut corporate jargon, but also cut goofy, cutesy, slang-heavy, low-register wording.

## Contract

- Prefer precise standard wording over corp-speak and over faux-friendly slop.
- Sound clear, adult, and direct.
- Use common words when they are accurate; use formal words when they are cleaner.
- Avoid MBA sludge, startup brochure verbs, meme phrasing, and app-copy baby talk.
- Do not make serious messages playful.
- Do not replace a strong term with a weaker one just to sound casual.
- Keep labels and status text short.
- Preserve domain precision where the exact term matters.

## Rewrite Test

Choose the option that is:

1. more precise
2. more standard
3. more compact
4. less gimmicky

If a rewrite sounds like a tooltip written by a marketer, a social-media intern, or a person trying to sound "relatable," reject it.

## Prefer This

Prefer `Transfer` over `Move`

Prefer `Thank you` over `Great, thanks`

Prefer `Discount` over `Deal` or `Steal`

Prefer `Upgraded` over `Boosted`

Prefer `Please wait` over `Hang tight`

Prefer `Withdrawal` over `Get cash`

Prefer `Settings` over `Preferences` when the UI convention is standard settings language

Prefer `Settings` over `Tweak` or `Options` when `Settings` is the established product term

Prefer `Sign up` over `Join now`

Prefer `Log out` over `Exit`

Prefer `Edit` over `Change`

Prefer `Save` over `Done`

Prefer `Submit` over `Send` when the action is a formal submission

Prefer `Upload` over `Add` when the action is specifically file upload

Prefer `Retry` over `Try again` when compact UI language is better

Prefer `Critical failure` over `Major hiccup`

Prefer `Unhandled exception` over `Unexpected issue` in technical contexts

Prefer `System crash` over `App stopped` in technical contexts

Prefer `Network error` over `Can't connect`

Prefer `Timeout` over `Took too long`

Prefer `Out of memory` over `Ran out of space`

Prefer `Disk full` over `No room left`

Prefer `Permission denied` over `Not allowed`

Prefer `File not found` over `Can't find it`

Prefer `Service unavailable` over `Down for now`

Prefer `Balance` over `Your total`

Prefer `Statement` over `Your activity`

Prefer `Loan` over `Borrow`

Prefer `Invoice` over `Your bill`

Prefer `Budget` over `Spending plan`

Prefer `Deadline` over `Due date` when the context is formal or operational

Prefer `Authenticate` over `Sign in` in technical documentation

Prefer `Authorize` over `Grant access` in technical documentation

Prefer `Bill of materials` over `Parts list` in engineering/manufacturing contexts

## Avoid

- corp-speak: `leverage`, `facilitate`, `utilize`, `solutioning`, `seamless experience`
- lexical slop: `hang tight`, `whoopsie`, `oops`, `got it!`, `never mind`, `down for now`
- hype words: `boosted`, `supercharge`, `unlock`, `game-changing`
- fake warmth in serious states: `major hiccup`, `app had a little problem`
- vague downgrade rewrites that remove useful meaning

## Guardrails

- UI copy may be softer than technical docs, but never childish.
- Error copy can be human, but must still expose the real problem.
- Prefer the platform's canonical term over novelty.
- When the exact technical term is user-facing and important, keep it.
- If two terms are both acceptable, prefer the more established one.
